Introduction to NMR

When atoms are placed in a magnetic field , the nuclei can ‘spin’ either with or against the magnetic field. A sample containing the desired compounds is inserted into a strong magnet during NMR spectroscopy. The sample is then exposed to radio waves, which cause the atomic nuclei to absorb energy and change orientation. When the radio waves are turned off, the nuclei return to their natural orientation and release the absorbed energy. This energy release is detected and analysed. Titan processes and analyses NMR data to help interpret complex spectra.

Biological NMR is a specialised application focused on molecules such as proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates and their interactions. The samples used in experiments are typically isotopically enriched, meaning that specific atoms within biomolecules have been changed to stable isotopes like carbon-13 and nitrogen-15. NMR signals can be read more easily by selectively labelling certain atoms.

What is Titan?

NMR TITAN stands out as a user-friendly software solution designed for the meticulous analysis of two-dimensional titration experiments' lineshapes. Through a meticulously calculated examination of chemical exchanges within 2D experiments, TITAN distinguishes itself by presenting a range of advantages compared to alternative analysis methods. It effectively eradicates various systematic errors, effectively resolves the challenges of overlapping peaks, and empowers the fitting of data to intricate binding models like dimerization, induced fit, or conformational selection. The Titan software, specifically tailored for NMR 2D lineshape analysis in titration experiments, offers a streamlined experience that includes quantum mechanical simulation of common pulse sequences and a selection of diverse binding models.

Why should you use Titan?

Noteworthy is Titan software's unique approach of generating simulations exclusively from the recorded peaks, diverging from conventional programs that estimate data by selecting peak positions—an approach susceptible to issues arising from overlaps. This innovative feature enables users to define their preferred regions of interest within spectra, efficiently addressing erroneous data stemming from overlaps. The software provides users with the flexibility to input their personalized estimates for chemical shift, dissociation constant, and dissociation rate constant. Titan then utilizes these user-defined values as a foundation to construct an appropriate model, promptly refining values until an optimal graph materializes.

Beyond its core capabilities, the software also furnishes users with essential error analysis tools. The inclusion of both Bootstrap and Jackknife error analysis functions adds an additional layer of credibility to data auditing processes. In essence, NMR TITAN emerges as a robust and accessible tool, catering to intricate 2D lineshape analysis in titration experiments, while its innovative features and user-friendly interface empower researchers to extract meaningful insights from their data with accuracy and confidence.
